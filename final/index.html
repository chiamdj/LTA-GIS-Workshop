    Final Assessment

    Instructions

    This final assessment constitutes 50% of your final grade.

    Data required (download from OneDrive\FinalAssessmentData.zip):

    • FinalAssessmentData.gdb containing
      • RapidTransitSystemStation_Point point feature class, containing the point locations of all train stations islandwide
      • Household polygon feature class (representing all the residential land use parcels in URA's MP19 Land Use Plan)
      • LTA_URA_WALK_CYCLE_NW_FD network dataset, containing a network dataset of the walking and cycling paths in Singapore

    Case Study

    Read the article from MOT below (source):

    Expanding Our Rail Network

    The size of our rail network will increase from about 230 km today, to about 360 km in 2030, so that it can reach more people and places. That will make the rail network density comparable to rail density in London and New York City and higher than that of Tokyo and Hong Kong today. More households will be served directly by the rail network, and about 8 in 10 households will be within a 10-minute walk of a train station.

    The rail network will be denser both within and outside the city. Almost everyone who lives or works in the city will be able to walk to an MRT station within five minutes. More areas outside the city, such as West Coast, Loyang, Jurong Industrial Estate and Punggol North, will also be connected to the rail network.

    The denser network will also provide more options and alternative travel routes to enhance the convenience of rail travel, and improve the network's resilience in the event of a disruption.

    Objective

    You have been tasked to identify the proportion of households that is within a 10-min walk of all train stations islandwide. Using the network dataset given, create a Service Area Analysis Layer in ArcGIS Pro to perform the following tasks:

    1. Identify the percentage of households that is currently within 800 metres walking distance (roughly 10-min walk) of any train station today. Round off your result to 3 decimal places.

      Hint

      You may calculate this value by taking the total area of households within 800 metres walking distance divided by the total area of the households feature class.

    2. Create a map of your result that will be presented to Senior Management. Save it as a PDF in 300 dpi and email it, together with your answer for Question 1, to lta_ignite_support@lta.gov.sg.

      Make sure you include the following elements in your map:

      • A3 size
      • LTA logo (available in OneDrive folder)
      • Security Classification (set as Restricted, Non-Sensitive)
      • Title
      • Your name and division
      • Legend
      • Scale bar
      • North arrow
      • Your answer for Question 1

      The following layers should be shown on your map (in the recommended order):

      • RapidTransitSystemStation_Point feature class (an MRT logo is available in the OneDrive folder)
      • Households within 10 minutes (in green)
      • Household polygon layer (in red)
      • Service Area of train stations
      • Ignite Colour Basemap or GDM Basemap

    3. Export your project as an ArcGIS Pro Project Package and email it to lta_ignite_support@lta.gov.sg.

    Assessment Criteria

    This assessment carries 50% of your final grade.

    Your project will be graded based on three components:

    • Use of the correct geoprocessing tools
    • Your final result for (a)
    • Map design and cartography
